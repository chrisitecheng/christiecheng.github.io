About

I'm familiar with the following programmes: Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, Dreamweaver, Muse and Sublime Text.Feel free to contact me if you're interested and thank you for taking the time to look through my portfolio!

Sailor Moon's 20th Anniversary

These are some of the objects crafted from various papers to form a stereographic poster meant to celebrate the 20th anniversary of Sailor Moon (2014). This was part of the final specialism project during my 1st year of study.

  • Client: LASALLE College of the Arts
  • Date: April 2014

Tag Heuer Helmet Design

We were approached by TAG Heuer to create helmet designs for Jenson Button, a British Formula One driver. As a team with my friend, Nicole, we decided to base the designs on the helmet on Button's love for Japanese culture. This image of the samurai parting waves is one of the many illustrations adorning the helmet and was sketched out via Adobe Illustrator.

  • Client: TAG Heuer
  • Date: August 2015

Cubiland's Request

This work was a commission by LINE Play avatar, Cubiland. She requested that I sketched out something based on how her avatar looked like, hence, the curls and the jewelled crown.

  • Client: Cubiland
  • Date: January 2014

Six Sketches - Alien Eyes

This is one of the six sketches we were meant to produce as part of practice for my illustration minor. The theme was "Sci-Fi", with the short story I received and had to depict having a lot of emphasis on the idea of eyes appearing like galaxies and being drawn into them.

  • Client: LASALLE College of the Arts
  • Date: March 2016

"Did I Have Fun?" Magazine

For the final project of Year 3 Sem 1, we had to design a magazine based on any topic we were interested in. Being an avid gamer and a huge fan of DOTA 2, I decided to base my magazine on the game, the contents revolving around the many aspects that comprise it. From fan art to updates, all contents within the magazine were carefully curated to bring across my desired outcome.

  • Client: LASALLE College of the Arts
  • Date: November 2015

Dragon Nest Costume Design

Dragon Nest SEA held a costume design contest in 2013, whereby players were free to create costumes for any class of their preference. I chose the academic and the theme for my design was "Spring". Basing the poses off of my character within the game, I repainted her digitally along with my costume's design.

  • Client: Dragon Nest SEA
  • Date: October 2013

Charles & Keith
Tote Bag Design

"Let dreams take flight" was the theme for this project, a collaboration between Charles & Keith and Club Rainbow. This design was one of a series of three, each containing a variation of objects one would have within their bags, according to their professions. The idea behind it was that every bag's design would be based upon three different children of Club Rainbow and their hopes for the future, the objects representing their future selves with fulfilled dreams.

  • Client: CHARLES & KEITH
  • Date: March 2016

Typography - Fairy Oaks (Base)

For the 2D module during the final semester of my foundation year, we had to craft our on typeface based on a list of poems given to us. The poem I chose was William Allingham's "The Fairies", which enraptured me with its powerful imagery. Since the poem was based on scenery in Ireland, I crafted the base serif font from oak trees, which are considered as sacred to the ancient Celts. After which, they were painted upon with watercolour, with details of the landscapes of Ireland.

  • Client: LASALLE College of the Arts
  • Date: April 2014

Six Sketches - Steampunk

For this theme, I chose to work with coloured pencils, so as to vary the outcomes of the three different themes.
